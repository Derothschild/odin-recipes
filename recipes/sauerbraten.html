Sauerbraten

Description: A plate of a savory German-Texas sauerbraten.

Ingredients

For the marinade:

  • 4-5 pounds beef roast
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 10-12 whold cloves
  • 10-12 whole black pepercorns
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon juniper berries
  • 1 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup filtered water
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For cooking:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

Instructions:

  1. In a large container, combine the onion, carrots, garlic, cloves, peppercorns, bay leaves, mustard seeds, juniper berries, red wine vinegar, water, red wine, brown sugar, and salt.
  2. Place the beef roast in the marinade, ensuring it's fully submerged. Cover the container and refrigerate for at least 2 to 3 days, turning the meat occasionally to evenly marinate.
  3. After marinating, remove the beef from the marinade and pat it dry with paper towels. Strain the marinade and set it aside.
  4. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the vegetable oil and butter over medium-high heat. Brown the beef roast on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  5. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent.
  6. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and cook for a couple of minutes, stirring constantly.
  7. Gradually add the strained marinade and beef broth to the pot, stirring to combine and create a smooth sauce.
  8. Return the beef to the pot. Cover and let it simmer on low heat for about 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  9. Remove the beef from the pot and cover it to keep warm.
  10. Strain the sauce to remove any solids and return it to the pot. Stir in the sour cream and heat gently without boiling.
  11. Slice the beef and serve it with the sauce.
